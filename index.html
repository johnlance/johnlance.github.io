Step 1: Ward (Build Emergency Fund)

Warding is your emergency fund. If you have $80,000 in student debt, $22,400 more on credit cards, and $8,200 in medical bills, doesn't matter. You gotta ward first.

If you're in lane as the junglers finish their clears, if you're pushing inhibs at 25:00, if you're setting up for a sneaky Baron, it doesn't matter. You gotta ward. Everybody knows it, nobody does it as much as they should. Wards aren't fun. They cost gold and time, the opponents always find and destroy them, and you can't even have multiple pinks out anymore (hold on, i'm hearing in my ear that this has been the case since season 4 and i am a thousand years old.)

But you know what happens without vision. I know you don't want to ward. I know there are cooler uses for that gold, and focusing on consistent wards delays you getting that Warmogs seemingly forever. But you know what puts you a lot further from the Warmogs? Spending 48 seconds looking at a black and white screen.

After getting a roof over your head and other necessities (including what you need to work), focus on building up your emergency fund. Get to the point that if you lose your job or your transportation breaks down, you aren't going to go into further debt while you recover. Think about how much you would spend in a month if you stopped making money. You'd be able to cut back on buying battle passes, but you can't really pay less rent or make a smaller car payment. You want to save somewhere between three and six months of expenses.
