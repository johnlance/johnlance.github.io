After getting a roof over your head and other necessities (including what you need to work), focus on building up your emergency fund. Get to the point that if you lose your job or your transportation breaks down, you aren't going to go into further debt while you recover. Think about how much you would spend in a month if you stopped making money. You'd be able to cut back on buying battle passes, but you can't really pay less rent or make a smaller car payment. You want to save somewhere between three and six months of expenses.

Think of retirement savings as your late-game insurance. You wouldn't enter a 40-minute game without considering your build path, would you? Similarly, you shouldn't go through your career without planning for retirement.

Step 4: Diversify Your Item Set (Invest Wisely)

A well-rounded champion build is key to victory. Similarly, a diverse investment portfolio is crucial for long-term financial success.

Explore Investment Strategies
‚öîÔ∏è Show More

Investing is like building your champion's defenses. You wouldn't stack only armor against a team with mixed damage, right? Just as you need both armor and magic resistance to protect against different types of threats, you need a mix of investments to guard against various market conditions.

Consider a diverse portfolio like a well-rounded item build:

  • Stocks: Your Infinity Edge. High risk, high potential reward.
  • Bonds: Your Thornmail. More defensive, offers steady returns.
  • Real Estate: Your Warmog's Armor. Slow, steady growth that compounds over time.
  • Cash/Savings: Your Refillable Potion. Not the most exciting, but provides liquidity when you need it.

As you get closer to your financial goals (late game), you might want to shift to a more defensive strategy. Just like how you might prioritize Guardian Angel when protecting your nexus in the final push.
